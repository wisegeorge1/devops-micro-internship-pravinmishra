#### Question 2 — What is AWS Free Tier, and how long does it last?

The AWS Free Tier is a program from Amazon Web Services (AWS) that lets you use selected AWS services at no cost, within specified usage limits. It's designed to help new users learn, build, and test applications without incurring charges. 

The free tier general last for 6 months with a $100 free credit on it that increases to $200 as you begin to use their services. So within the 6 months period you are not expected to use resources that exceed $200 in total. The account closes on its own 6 months after you open it or when your credits run out.

---

#### Question 3 — Name three AWS Free Tier services and their free usage limits.

1. AWS EC2 (Elastic Cloud Compute)
   Free limit: Up to 750 hours per month of eligible EC2 instance (like t2.micro, t3.micro, or t4g.micro) usage for the first 6 months after creating an AWS account.

2. AWS S3 (Simple Storage Service)
   Amazon S3 includes an "Always Free" allocation of 5 GB of Standard Storage, 20,000 GET requests, and 2,000 PUT/COPY/POST/LIST requests per month

3. AWS Lambda
   Includes 1 million free requests per month and 400,000 GB-seconds of compute time every month under the Always Free tier.
